# Portfolio Profitability Overview

The **combined ratio of 172.54%** indicates that for every **£1 of premium earned**, the insurer is paying approximately:

- **£1.43 in claims**
- **£0.29 in operational expenses**

This leaves the portfolio operating at a **significant underwriting loss**.

Typical industry benchmarks for combined ratios:

| Combined Ratio | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| < 90% | Highly profitable |
| 90–100% | Sustainable profitability |
| 100–110% | Marginal profitability |
| > 110% | Unprofitable portfolio |

A combined ratio above **170% represents severe underwriting losses**, suggesting systemic issues with pricing, risk selection, or claims management.

# Average Claim Cost by Property Type

The **Average Claim Cost by Property Type** visual highlights which property categories produce the most severe claims.

### Maisonettes – Highest Severity Risk

Maisonettes demonstrate the **highest average claim cost**, approaching approximately **£180K–£200K per claim**.

Possible explanations include:

- Structural complexity in multi-level units
- Shared structural components
- Older building stock
- Higher repair complexity

Although maisonettes may represent a smaller portion of policies, they create **significant financial exposure per claim**.

---

### Bungalows – High Claim Severity

Bungalows show the **second highest average claim cost**.

Possible drivers include:

- Larger structural footprint
- Higher rebuild cost per property
- Higher exposure to subsidence and structural damage

---

### Flats – Moderate Severity

Flats exhibit **moderate claim severity**.

Although rebuild costs per unit are lower, flats can still generate complex claims due to:

- shared building infrastructure
- water leakage between units
- legal and structural repair complexity

---

### Houses – Lowest Average Claim Cost

Houses show the **lowest average claim cost per property type**.

This may be due to:

- a larger distribution of policies
- more predictable structural layouts
- more modern housing developments

However, houses still represent **significant aggregate risk due to portfolio concentration**.

# Strategic Risk Mitigation Recommendations

Based on the profitability and risk segmentation analysis, several strategic actions could improve portfolio performance.

### Improve Risk-Based Pricing

Premium pricing should better reflect:

- property type risk
- rebuild cost exposure
- geographic risk factors
- claim severity trends

---

### Tighten Underwriting Criteria

Stricter underwriting standards may be required for:

- high rebuild value properties
- older building structures
- regions with high claim activity

---

### Rebalance Distribution Channels

Reducing reliance on high-risk acquisition channels such as aggregators could improve portfolio quality.

Expanding direct channels may provide **greater control over underwriting standards**.

---

### Reduce High-Risk Property Exposure

Monitoring and managing the concentration of high-risk properties may reduce portfolio volatility.

This could include:

- premium adjustments
- policy limits
- enhanced property inspections

# Conclusion

The Profitability & Risk Segmentation analysis reveals that the current insurance portfolio is **operating at a significant underwriting loss**.

Key findings include:

- Combined ratio exceeding **170%**
- High claim severity in maisonettes and bungalows
- High proportion of high-risk properties
- Elevated claim frequency in aggregator channels
- Premium pricing that does not adequately reflect risk exposure

Addressing these issues through improved underwriting discipline, risk-based pricing, and distribution channel management will be essential to restoring portfolio profitability.
