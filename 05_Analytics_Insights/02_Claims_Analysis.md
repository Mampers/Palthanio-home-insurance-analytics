# Claims Frequency by Claim Type

The **Claims Frequency by Claim Type** visual highlights which claim types occur most often.

### Escape of Water

Escape of water claims represent the **highest frequency claim category** within the portfolio.

Possible reasons include:

- Burst pipes
- Plumbing failures
- Appliance leaks
- Property maintenance issues

Water-related claims are common in home insurance due to their **high frequency and moderate severity**.

These types of claims often occur in:

- Older housing infrastructure
- Poorly maintained properties
- Colder winter climates

---

### Accidental Damage

Accidental damage claims are the **second most frequent claim category**.

These claims typically involve:

- Internal household damage
- Broken fixtures
- Furniture damage

Although frequent, these claims usually have **lower financial severity compared to structural damage claims**.

---

### Theft

Theft claims show **moderate claim frequency**.

Typical drivers include:

- Burglary
- Opportunistic theft
- High population density areas

Urban properties generally experience higher theft exposure.

---

### Storm Claims

Storm claims have **lower frequency but can cause high severity events**, particularly during extreme weather periods.

Examples include:

- Roof damage
- Fallen trees
- Structural damage from wind

---

### Fire Claims

Fire claims appear to have **low frequency but extremely high severity**.

Fire incidents tend to generate:

- Significant structural damage
- High rebuild costs
- Extended claims settlement periods

---

# Average Claim Cost by Claim Type

The **Average Claim Cost by Claim Type** visual provides insight into claim severity.

### Fire Claims – Highest Severity

Fire claims show the **highest average claim cost**, approaching approximately **£300,000 per claim**.

This reflects the catastrophic nature of fire incidents, which can require:

- Full structural rebuild
- Extensive repairs
- Temporary accommodation costs

Although fire claims occur infrequently, they represent **major financial risk exposure**.

---

### Subsidence Claims – Very High Severity

Subsidence claims also demonstrate **extremely high average claim cost**, often exceeding **£200,000 per claim**.

Subsidence typically involves:

- Structural foundation movement
- Ground instability
- Expensive structural engineering repairs

These claims are **rare but very costly**, making them critical risk factors.

---

### Flood Claims – Moderate to High Severity

Flood claims demonstrate **moderately high average costs**, reflecting damage caused by water intrusion and property contamination.

Common flood damage includes:

- Structural repairs
- Flooring replacement
- Electrical system damage

---

### Lower Severity Claims

Claim types such as:

- Theft
- Storm
- Accidental damage
- Escape of water

generally show **lower average claim cost** compared to catastrophic claim types.

However, their **higher frequency still contributes significantly to overall claims cost**.

---

# Claims Cost by Distribution Channel

The **Claims Cost by Distribution Channel** visual highlights which sales channels generate the largest claims costs.

### Broker Channel – Highest Claims Cost

The broker channel contributes the **largest share of claims cost**, significantly exceeding other distribution channels.

Possible explanations include:

- Brokers selling a larger proportion of policies
- Higher risk policies being distributed via brokers
- Brokers targeting complex or high-value properties

This may indicate a **risk concentration in broker-distributed policies**.

---

### Direct Channel

Direct sales channels show **moderate claims costs**, which may indicate:

- More controlled underwriting
- Better customer risk segmentation
- Lower-risk policyholder profiles

---

### Aggregator Channel

Aggregator platforms generate **similar claims costs to direct channels**, though potentially with slightly higher risk exposure.

Aggregators often compete heavily on price, which may lead to:

- Underpriced policies
- Higher risk customer segments

---

# Claims Cost by Property Type

The **Claims Cost by Property Type** visual demonstrates how property characteristics influence claims exposure.

### Houses – Largest Claims Exposure

Houses represent the **largest share of claims cost** in the portfolio.

Possible explanations include:

- Houses make up the largest portion of insured properties
- Houses have larger structures and rebuild values
- Detached or semi-detached homes face higher structural risk

Houses generally carry **higher rebuild costs compared to smaller property types**.

---

### Flats – Second Largest Exposure

Flats contribute a smaller but still notable portion of claims cost.

Potential factors include:

- Shared building structures
- Water leakage between units
- High-density living environments

---

### Bungalows and Maisonettes

These property types represent **smaller segments of the portfolio**, contributing less to overall claims cost.

However, they may still represent **specific underwriting risks depending on location and construction style**.

---

# Risk Observations

Several important risk observations can be derived from this claims analysis.

### High Frequency Claims

Escape of water and accidental damage claims occur most frequently.

These claim types represent **operational cost drivers** and may benefit from preventative measures.

---

### High Severity Claims

Fire and subsidence claims show extremely high average costs.

Although rare, they represent **catastrophic loss exposure**.

These claims can significantly distort portfolio profitability.

---

### Distribution Channel Risk

Broker-distributed policies appear to generate the highest claims costs.

This may indicate:

- Riskier property profiles
- Higher value insured assets
- Different underwriting standards across channels

---

### Property Type Exposure

Houses dominate claims exposure due to their:

- Larger insured value
- Greater structural complexity
- Higher rebuild cost

---

# Strategic Recommendations

Based on the claims analysis, several strategic improvements could be considered.

### Strengthen Underwriting Controls

Introduce stricter underwriting criteria for:

- High-value properties
- Subsidence-prone regions
- Older housing stock

---

### Improve Risk-Based Pricing

Premium pricing should reflect claim severity risks, particularly for:

- Fire exposure
- Subsidence risk
- Flood-prone areas

---

### Preventative Risk Programs

Encourage policyholders to implement preventative measures such as:

- Leak detection systems
- Structural inspections
- Flood prevention barriers

---

### Monitor Broker Portfolio Risk

Review the underwriting standards of broker-distributed policies to ensure risk profiles remain within acceptable limits.

---

# Conclusion

The Claims Analysis dashboard reveals several important insights:

- Escape of water claims drive **high frequency claims activity**
- Fire and subsidence claims represent **major severity risks**
- Broker channels generate the **largest share of claims costs**
- Houses dominate **portfolio exposure**

Addressing these factors through improved underwriting, pricing adjustments, and preventative risk management strategies could significantly improve overall portfolio profitability.
