# Policy Portfolio Analysis – Insurance Portfolio Insights

This section analyses the **composition and financial characteristics of the insured policy portfolio**. The dashboard provides insights into policy distribution, property exposure, rebuild costs, and premium generation across property types and distribution channels.

The purpose of this analysis is to understand **where premium revenue originates, the exposure level of insured properties, and potential risk concentration within the portfolio**.

---

---

# Active Policies by Property Type

The **Active Policies by Property Type** visual shows the distribution of policies across different residential property categories.

### Houses – Dominant Property Segment

Houses represent the **largest portion of the insured portfolio**, accounting for the majority of active policies.

Possible reasons include:

- Houses being the most common residential property type
- Higher insurance demand for detached or semi-detached homes
- Greater rebuild exposure compared to smaller dwellings

Because houses represent the majority of policies, they also represent the **largest exposure to potential claims losses**.

---

### Flats – Second Largest Segment

Flats represent the **second largest segment of insured properties**.

This segment typically shows:

- Lower rebuild costs per unit
- Higher density urban locations
- Potential shared building risks

Flats may experience different risk profiles compared to houses, particularly in urban areas where property values and population density are higher.

---

### Bungalows and Maisonettes

Bungalows and maisonettes represent **smaller portions of the portfolio**.

These property types may carry different risk characteristics such as:

- Older property construction
- Unique structural layouts
- Location-specific risk factors

However, due to their smaller share of policies, they contribute less to overall portfolio exposure.

---

# Average Rebuild Cost by Property Type

The **Average Rebuild Cost by Property Type** visual highlights the structural cost exposure of each property category.

### Bungalows – Highest Rebuild Cost

Bungalows appear to have the **highest average rebuild cost**, approaching approximately £180K–£190K.

Possible explanations include:

- Larger land footprint
- Single-floor structural design requiring larger foundations
- Higher construction costs

---

### Houses – High Structural Exposure

Houses also show **high rebuild costs**, averaging approximately £170K per property.

Because houses dominate the portfolio, this creates a **significant total financial exposure**.

---

### Maisonettes and Flats – Lower Rebuild Costs

Maisonettes and flats show comparatively lower rebuild costs.

This is expected because:

- Shared structures reduce individual rebuild cost
- Smaller living areas
- Multi-unit buildings spread construction costs

However, despite lower individual rebuild values, flats can still create **significant aggregate exposure due to high density**.

---

# Premium by Distribution Channel

The **Premium by Distribution Channel** visual shows how insurance policies are sold.

### Broker Channel – Largest Premium Source

The broker channel generates **the largest share of premium revenue**, accounting for over half of total policies sold.

Possible reasons include:

- Broker networks targeting higher value properties
- Brokers specialising in complex insurance needs
- Higher customer trust through intermediaries

However, reliance on brokers can introduce potential risks such as:

- commission costs
- risk selection variability
- inconsistent underwriting standards

---

### Direct Channel

Direct sales generate the **second highest premium contribution**.

Direct channels often attract:

- lower-risk customers
- digitally engaged policyholders
- more price-sensitive customers

Direct channels can offer **higher profit margins due to reduced intermediary costs**.

---

### Aggregator Channel

Aggregator platforms generate a smaller portion of premium revenue.

Aggregator customers often prioritise:

- price comparisons
- fast policy purchases
- competitive pricing

This can create potential risks such as:

- thinner underwriting margins
- higher churn rates

---

# Total Net Premium by Property Type

The **Total Net Premium by Property Type** treemap highlights which property categories generate the most revenue.

### Houses – Largest Revenue Contributor

Houses generate the **largest share of premium income**.

This aligns with their dominance in:

- policy count
- rebuild value
- insured exposure

Because houses generate the most premium, they also represent the **largest potential claims exposure**.

---

### Flats – Moderate Revenue Contribution

Flats generate a moderate share of premium revenue.

Although rebuild costs are lower per property, the **large number of flats still contributes meaningful premium income**.

---

### Smaller Segments

Bungalows and maisonettes contribute smaller shares of premium revenue due to:

- lower policy counts
- smaller portfolio representation

# Portfolio Exposure

The portfolio shows significant structural exposure.

### Total Portfolio Rebuild Value

The total insured rebuild value exceeds **£1.06 billion**, representing the financial liability the insurer faces if catastrophic property losses occur.

This metric is critical for:

- capital reserve planning
- catastrophe modelling
- regulatory compliance

---

# Pricing Adequacy

The **Average Premium per Rebuild ratio of 0.71%** suggests that for every £100 of property rebuild value, the insurer collects approximately **£0.71 in premium**.

This ratio may indicate potential **underpricing relative to exposure**, especially when considering the high loss ratio observed in the Executive Summary.

---

# Distribution Risk Concentration

With **over 51% of policies sold through brokers**, the portfolio shows strong reliance on intermediary distribution channels.

While brokers provide access to a large customer base, this also introduces:

- dependency risk
- commission expenses
- potential underwriting variability

A more balanced distribution strategy may improve long-term portfolio stability.

---

# Strategic Observations

Several strategic insights can be derived from this portfolio analysis.

### High Property Exposure

The insurer is exposed to **over £1 billion in rebuild risk**, which requires careful underwriting and catastrophe modelling.

---

### Property Type Concentration

Houses dominate both policy count and premium revenue, making them the **primary driver of portfolio risk**.

---

### Distribution Channel Dependence

Heavy reliance on brokers may create:

- higher operational costs
- potential risk selection issues

---

### Pricing vs Exposure

The premium-to-rebuild ratio suggests that pricing may not fully reflect the structural exposure of insured properties.

---

# Strategic Recommendations

Based on the portfolio analysis, the insurer may consider several actions.

### Improve Risk-Based Pricing

Adjust premium pricing to better reflect:

- rebuild costs
- property type risks
- geographic exposure

---

### Diversify Distribution Channels

Reducing reliance on brokers may improve margins and control over underwriting.

Potential strategies include:

- expanding direct digital channels
- improving customer acquisition marketing

---

### Strengthen Portfolio Diversification

Reducing concentration in certain property types may help manage risk exposure.

For example:

- increasing flat-based policies
- diversifying into different housing categories

---

### Monitor Structural Exposure

Given the £1.06Bn rebuild exposure, the insurer should maintain strong:

- catastrophe modelling
- capital adequacy planning
- reinsurance strategies

---

# Conclusion

The Policy Portfolio Analysis dashboard provides important insights into how the insurance portfolio is structured.

Key observations include:

- Houses dominate policy count and premium generation
- Brokers generate over half of premium income
- The insurer carries over £1Bn in structural rebuild exposure
- Pricing relative to rebuild cost may be insufficient

Understanding these dynamics is critical for improving underwriting profitability and managing long-term insurance risk.
