# Claims Severity & Risk Drivers – Portfolio Risk Analysis

This section analyses the **severity of insurance claims and the underlying drivers of claim costs** within the home insurance portfolio. The objective of this dashboard is to identify how claim values are distributed, which claim types generate the largest financial impact, and which property characteristics contribute most to severe losses.

Understanding claim severity is critical for insurers because **a small number of high-cost claims can disproportionately impact profitability**. By analysing claim cost bands, claim types, and property characteristics, insurers can better manage risk exposure and improve underwriting decisions.

# Claims Distribution by Claim Cost Band

The **Claims by Claim Cost Band** visual illustrates how claims are distributed across different claim value ranges.

### Low Value Claims (£0K – £5K)

The majority of claims fall within the **£0K–£5K range**, indicating that most claims are relatively small in value.

Typical examples include:

- minor water damage
- small accidental damage claims
- minor repair costs

Although these claims occur frequently, their financial impact on the overall portfolio is relatively limited.

---

### Medium Value Claims (£5K – £20K)

A smaller portion of claims falls within the **£5K–£20K range**, representing moderate severity events such as:

- partial structural repairs
- appliance damage
- medium-scale water leaks

These claims contribute more significantly to overall claims costs.

---

### High Value Claims (£50K+)

High-value claims are relatively rare but financially significant.

Only **21 claims exceed £50,000**, representing **1.5% of all claims**, yet they contribute **14.02% of total claims cost**.

This demonstrates a classic insurance risk pattern where **a small number of catastrophic claims drive a disproportionate share of losses**.

---

# Total Claims Paid by Claim Type

The **Total Claims Paid by Claim Type** visual highlights which claim categories generate the greatest financial impact.

### Escape of Water – Largest Financial Driver

Escape of water claims represent the **largest contributor to claims costs**.

This is common in home insurance portfolios due to:

- burst pipes
- plumbing failures
- water leakage between properties
- appliance failures

Although individual water claims may not be catastrophic, their **high frequency creates significant aggregate cost**.

---

### Storm Damage

Storm-related claims represent the **second largest driver of claims costs**.

Weather-related claims often involve:

- roof damage
- structural damage
- fallen trees

Climate variability and extreme weather events can increase the frequency and severity of these claims.

---

### Accidental Damage

Accidental damage claims contribute a moderate share of total claims cost.

These claims typically involve:

- broken fixtures
- interior damage
- accidental household damage

Although frequent, these claims usually have **lower average cost per claim**.

---

### Theft

Theft claims are another notable contributor to claims cost.

Urban areas with higher population density often experience **higher theft-related insurance claims**.

---

### Flood, Fire and Subsidence

Although these claims occur less frequently, they often involve **higher repair costs** and more complex claims processing.

These types of claims can represent **catastrophic risk exposure**.

---

Medium severity claims average approximately **£40K–£50K**.

Examples include:

- storm damage requiring major repairs

---

### Low Severity Claims

Low severity claims generally cost **below £20K**, representing the majority of claim events.

These typically include:

- minor repairs

Pre-war properties demonstrate **the highest claim frequency**, exceeding **300 claims per 1000 policies**.

Older properties often carry higher risk due to:

- aging infrastructure
- outdated plumbing systems
- structural deterioration

---

### Mid-Century Properties (1945–1979)

Mid-century homes show **slightly lower but still elevated claims frequency**.

Many homes built during this period may require ongoing maintenance due to aging building materials.

Modern properties show **moderate claims frequency**, benefiting from:

- improved building standards
- better structural integrity
- modern plumbing systems

---

### New Builds (2000+)

- modern construction techniques

# Strategic Risk Mitigation Recommendations

Based on the claims severity analysis, insurers may consider several strategic actions.

### Preventative Risk Programs

Encourage policyholders to install:

- leak detection systems
- smart water shut-off valves
- structural inspection programs

---

### Property Age Risk Pricing

Premium pricing may need adjustment for older properties with higher risk profiles.

---

### Catastrophic Risk Management

Insurers should strengthen catastrophe modelling for events such as:

- floods
- storms
- structural fires

---

### Claims Prevention Initiatives

Educating policyholders on property maintenance and risk prevention could reduce claims frequency.

The Claims Severity & Risk Drivers dashboard highlights the structural drivers behind the insurer’s claims costs.
