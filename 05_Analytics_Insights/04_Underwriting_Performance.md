# Underwriting Performance – Insurance Portfolio Analysis

This section evaluates the **underwriting performance of the home insurance portfolio** by analysing claim severity, loss ratios, and distribution channel effectiveness. The objective is to determine whether the insurer is pricing policies appropriately relative to risk exposure and to identify segments that are driving underwriting losses.

Underwriting performance is assessed using several key indicators including **loss ratio, claims frequency, average claim cost, and distribution channel performance**.

# Average Claim Cost by Property Type

The **Average Claim Cost by Property Type** visual highlights the severity of claims across different residential property types.

### Maisonettes – Highest Claim Severity

Maisonettes exhibit the **highest average claim cost within the portfolio**.

Possible contributing factors include:

- Structural complexity in multi-level residential units
- Shared building components
- Higher repair costs in older constructions

Although maisonettes may not represent the largest share of policies, their **high claim severity increases overall financial risk**.

---

### Bungalows – High Severity Claims

Bungalows also demonstrate **high average claim costs**, which may be driven by:

- Larger ground floor footprints
- Higher rebuild costs due to larger structural foundations
- Older property stock

These properties often require **extensive structural repair in the event of major damage**.

---

### Flats – Moderate Severity

Flats show **moderate claim costs**, typically lower than houses or bungalows.

However, flats often involve **shared infrastructure**, which can create:

- complex claims involving multiple units
- higher legal and repair complexity

---

### Houses – Lower Average Claim Cost

Houses appear to have **lower average claim cost relative to other property types**.

This may be due to:

- broader distribution of house policies
- more predictable structural layouts
- better maintenance standards in newer housing developments

---

# Loss Ratio by Property Type

The **Loss Ratio by Property Type** visual provides insight into underwriting profitability for each property segment.

### Maisonettes – Highest Loss Ratio

Maisonettes demonstrate the **highest loss ratio**, indicating claims costs exceed premiums by the largest margin in this category.

This suggests that:

- pricing may not fully reflect risk exposure
- claims severity may be underestimated
- underwriting criteria may require tightening

---

### Houses and Bungalows – Significant Loss Ratios

Houses and bungalows also show **loss ratios exceeding 120%**, indicating that claims costs significantly exceed the premium collected.

Because houses represent the largest share of the portfolio, this category has the **greatest impact on overall underwriting profitability**.

---

### Flats – Slightly Lower Loss Ratio

Flats show a slightly lower loss ratio compared to other property types, though it still exceeds 100%.

This suggests that even the most stable property segment is **still generating underwriting losses**.

---

# Claims Frequency by Distribution Channel

The **Claims Frequency by Distribution Channel** visual highlights how often claims occur across different policy distribution methods.

### Aggregator Channel – Highest Claims Frequency

Aggregator platforms show the **highest claim frequency**, approaching approximately 10%.

Possible reasons include:

- price-driven customer acquisition
- higher risk customer segments
- limited underwriting control during purchase

Aggregator channels often prioritise **low premiums and quick policy purchase**, which can lead to higher risk exposure.

---

### Broker Channel – Moderate Frequency

Broker-sold policies show **moderately high claim frequency**.

Although brokers provide valuable market access, they may also sell policies to customers with **complex or higher-risk property profiles**.

---

### Direct Channel – Lower Frequency

Direct sales channels appear to have **slightly lower claims frequency**.

This may indicate:

- stronger underwriting controls
- better customer risk segmentation
- more selective pricing strategies

---

# Underwriting Performance by Distribution Channel

The **Underwriting Performance by Distribution Channel** visual compares premium generation with loss ratios across channels.

### Broker Channel – Largest Premium Contribution

The broker channel generates the **largest share of premium revenue**, approximately £3M.

However, the loss ratio associated with this channel remains above **120%**, indicating that broker-distributed policies are not profitable.

---

### Aggregator Channel – High Loss Ratio

Aggregator channels show **very high loss ratios**, potentially exceeding **160%**.

Although premium volume is smaller, the high claims cost relative to premium suggests that these policies are **particularly unprofitable**.

---

### Direct Channel – Moderate Performance

Direct channels generate **moderate premium volumes with slightly lower loss ratios**.

This suggests that direct underwriting processes may provide better risk control.

---

# Underwriting Risk Observations

Several important underwriting risks emerge from this analysis.

### Structural Loss Across All Segments

All property types demonstrate **loss ratios above 100%**, meaning that underwriting losses exist across the entire portfolio.

This indicates a **systemic pricing or risk selection issue**.

---

### High Severity Property Segments

Maisonettes and bungalows show **particularly high claim severity**, which may require targeted underwriting strategies.

---

### Channel-Based Risk

Aggregator and broker channels show **higher claims frequency and loss ratios**, suggesting weaker risk filtering during policy acquisition.

---

### Pricing vs Claims Severity

Premium pricing does not appear to fully reflect the **severity of claims across property types**, contributing to the elevated loss ratios.

---

# Strategic Underwriting Recommendations

Based on the underwriting performance analysis, several strategic actions could improve profitability.

### Strengthen Risk-Based Pricing

Premium pricing should reflect:

- property type risk
- rebuild cost exposure
- geographic risk factors

---

### Improve Underwriting Controls

Additional underwriting checks may be required for:

- high rebuild value properties
- older housing stock
- properties with historical claim activity

---

### Rebalance Distribution Channels

Reducing dependence on high-risk acquisition channels such as aggregators could improve portfolio quality.

Expanding direct sales channels may provide greater underwriting control.

---

### Introduce Preventative Risk Measures

Encouraging policyholders to adopt preventative measures such as:

- leak detection systems
- structural inspections
- storm protection measures

could reduce claims frequency.

---

# Conclusion

The Underwriting Performance analysis reveals that the insurer's portfolio is currently **operating at a significant underwriting loss**.

Key findings include:

- Loss ratios exceeding 120% across most property types
- High claim severity in maisonettes and bungalows
- Higher claim frequency within aggregator and broker distribution channels
- Premium pricing that does not adequately reflect property risk exposure

Improving underwriting discipline, adjusting pricing strategies, and rebalancing distribution channels will be critical to restoring portfolio profitability.
