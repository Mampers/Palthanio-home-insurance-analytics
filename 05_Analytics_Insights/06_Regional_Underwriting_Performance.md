# Regional Underwriting Performance – Portfolio Risk Analysis

This section analyses **underwriting performance across geographic regions** within the insurance portfolio. The objective is to identify regional variations in claims behaviour, profitability, and underwriting risk exposure.

Regional analysis is critical in insurance because **geographic factors strongly influence risk**, including:

- weather exposure
- property values
- population density
- infrastructure age
- regional crime levels
- environmental risks

By analysing **loss ratios, claims frequency, claim severity, and premium distribution by region**, insurers can identify areas that are generating underwriting losses and adjust pricing or underwriting policies accordingly.

# Highest Regional Loss Ratios

The **Highest Regional Loss Ratios** visual highlights which geographic regions are generating the largest underwriting losses.

### London – Highest Loss Ratio

London shows the **highest loss ratio across all regions**, exceeding 200%.

Possible drivers include:

- higher property values
- higher rebuild costs
- greater population density
- higher theft or accidental damage risk

Urban areas such as London typically carry **higher severity claims due to expensive property repairs**.

---

### Scotland and Wales – Elevated Loss Ratios

Scotland and Wales also demonstrate **high loss ratios**, indicating underwriting challenges in these regions.

Potential contributing factors may include:

- severe weather exposure
- rural infrastructure risks
- older housing stock

Regions with older property structures often experience **higher maintenance-related claims**.

---

### Northern Regions – Moderate to High Loss Ratios

Regions such as:

- North East
- North West
- West Midlands

show moderately high loss ratios, suggesting that underwriting profitability is strained but slightly more stable than in London.

These areas may still experience:

- structural property risks
- weather-related damage
- regional economic factors affecting property maintenance.

---

# Regional Claims Frequency

The **Regional Claims Frequency** visual shows how often claims occur relative to policy count in each region.

### London – Highest Claims Frequency

London shows the **highest claims frequency**, approaching approximately **350 claims per 1000 policies**.

This suggests:

- higher population density
- more insured properties
- increased exposure to theft and accidental damage.

Urban environments typically experience **higher claim occurrence due to activity density**.

---

### Wales and Northern Regions

Regions such as Wales, North West, and North East show **moderately high claims frequency**.

These regions may experience:

- weather-related claims
- structural damage claims
- regional environmental risks.

---

### Eastern Regions – Lower Frequency

Regions such as East Midlands and East of England show **lower claims frequency**, suggesting relatively stable underwriting environments.

Lower claim frequency may indicate:

- newer housing developments
- lower population density
- reduced environmental risk exposure.

The West Midlands also demonstrates relatively high claim severity.

# Strategic Regional Underwriting Recommendations

Based on the regional underwriting performance analysis, several strategic improvements could be considered.

### Implement Regional Risk Pricing

Premium pricing should be adjusted to reflect:

- geographic risk exposure
- claim frequency patterns
- rebuild cost differences.

---

### Strengthen Regional Underwriting Guidelines

Additional underwriting checks may be required in regions with elevated loss ratios.

For example:

- property inspections
- stricter rebuild value assessments
- regional underwriting restrictions.

---

### Improve Catastrophe Risk Modelling

Regions with high severity claims should incorporate stronger catastrophe modelling.

This includes modelling for:

- flood risk
- storm exposure
- subsidence risk.

---

### Balance Geographic Portfolio Exposure

Reducing concentration in high-loss regions may improve portfolio stability.

Diversifying the geographic distribution of policies can reduce volatility.

---

# Conclusion

The **Regional Underwriting Performance dashboard** highlights significant geographic variation in underwriting risk.

Key findings include:

- East of England shows the highest claim severity.
- Several regions demonstrate structural underwriting losses.
- Premium pricing may not adequately reflect regional risk exposure.

Improving regional pricing strategies, strengthening underwriting criteria, and balancing geographic exposure will be essential steps toward restoring profitability across the insurance portfolio.
