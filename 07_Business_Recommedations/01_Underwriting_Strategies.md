# Underwriting Strategy

## Overview

Underwriting strategy defines how an insurance company evaluates, prices, and accepts risk when issuing policies.  
The goal of underwriting is to ensure that **premiums charged adequately reflect the level of risk associated with each policy**, allowing the insurer to maintain profitability while remaining competitive in the market.

The insights generated from the Palthanio Home Insurance Analytics project reveal several areas where underwriting strategy can be improved.

---

# Key Underwriting Challenges Identified

Analysis of the dashboards revealed several underwriting concerns:

| Issue | Impact |
|------|------|
| Loss ratios exceeding 100% | Claims costs exceed premium income |
| High-risk property segments | Certain property types generate disproportionate claims |
| Regional underwriting losses | Specific regions show elevated claims frequency |
| Older properties | Higher claims frequency due to aging infrastructure |

These findings highlight the need for **risk-based underwriting improvements**.

---

# Strategic Underwriting Improvements

## Risk-Based Pricing

Premium pricing should reflect the underlying risk associated with each policy.

Risk factors to consider include:

- property rebuild cost
- property age
- geographic risk exposure
- historical claims behaviour
- environmental risk factors

Risk-based pricing ensures that **high-risk policies generate sufficient premium revenue** to cover potential claims.

---

## Property Age Adjustments

Older properties demonstrate higher claims frequency due to structural deterioration and outdated infrastructure.

Recommended underwriting actions:

- require property condition disclosures
- adjust premiums for older property age bands
- introduce additional underwriting checks for properties built before modern building standards

---

## Rebuild Cost Validation

Rebuild costs significantly influence claim severity.

Underwriting teams should ensure:

- rebuild values are accurately estimated
- policyholders do not under-insure or over-insure properties
- high-value properties are assessed carefully before policy issuance

Accurate rebuild cost evaluation reduces exposure to **large claim payouts**.

---

## High-Risk Property Segmentation

The analytics project identifies property segments that generate elevated claims costs.

Underwriting strategies should include:

- stricter risk evaluation for high-risk property types
- adjusted pricing tiers for different property categories
- selective underwriting in high-loss segments

---

# Expected Benefits

Improving underwriting strategy can produce the following benefits:

| Benefit | Impact |
|------|------|
| Improved loss ratio | Reduced underwriting losses |
| Better risk selection | Policies better aligned with risk |
| Stronger portfolio stability | Reduced volatility in claims costs |

---

# Conclusion

A well-designed underwriting strategy ensures that insurance companies **balance growth with risk management**.

By implementing risk-based pricing, stronger underwriting rules, and property risk segmentation, insurers can improve portfolio profitability while maintaining a competitive product offering.
