# Business Recommendations

## Overview

The **Business Recommendations** section translates analytical insights from the Palthanio Home Insurance Analytics dashboards into **strategic business decisions**.  
While the dashboards identify trends and risks across the insurance portfolio, this section focuses on **what actions the insurance company should take to improve profitability, reduce risk exposure, and optimise underwriting performance**.

These recommendations are based on insights derived from the following analytical areas:

- underwriting performance
- claims behaviour
- portfolio composition
- regional risk exposure
- claims severity drivers

The goal is to demonstrate how **data analytics supports business strategy and operational decision-making** within an insurance organisation.

---

# Key Strategic Objectives

The recommendations aim to address three primary objectives:

| Objective | Description |
|---|---|
| Improve Portfolio Profitability | Reduce underwriting losses and improve the combined ratio |
| Reduce Risk Exposure | Identify and mitigate high-risk policies, properties, and regions |
| Optimise Pricing Strategy | Ensure premiums accurately reflect underlying risk |

---

# 1. Underwriting Strategy

## Problem Identified

Analysis of the **Underwriting Performance dashboard** shows that the portfolio is operating at a **loss ratio above 100%**, meaning claims costs exceed earned premiums.

Certain segments of the portfolio, particularly specific **property types and distribution channels**, demonstrate higher loss ratios.

## Recommended Actions

### Refine Risk-Based Pricing

Premiums should be adjusted based on risk factors such as:

- property age
- geographic region
- claims history
- rebuild cost exposure

Risk-adjusted pricing ensures that **high-risk policies generate adequate premium income**.

### Strengthen Underwriting Rules

Underwriters may introduce stricter acceptance criteria for policies with elevated risk indicators.

Examples include:

- properties with high rebuild costs
- properties located in high-loss regions
- older buildings with higher claims frequency

---

# 2. Claims Cost Reduction

## Problem Identified

Claims analysis shows that **Escape of Water and Storm damage claims represent the largest drivers of claims cost**.

Although individual claims may not always be catastrophic, their **high frequency significantly impacts total claims expenditure**.

## Recommended Actions

### Introduce Preventative Risk Programs

Insurance companies can reduce claims by encouraging policyholders to adopt preventative technologies, including:

- smart leak detection systems
- automatic water shutoff valves
- improved property maintenance programs

### Incentivise Risk Prevention

Premium discounts can be offered to policyholders who install preventative systems, reducing the likelihood of expensive claims.

---

# 3. Portfolio Risk Management

## Problem Identified

Portfolio analysis indicates that **certain property segments and geographic regions contribute disproportionately to underwriting losses**.

Risk concentration within specific areas can create financial instability for the insurer.

## Recommended Actions

### Diversify Portfolio Risk

Reducing exposure to high-risk segments can stabilise the portfolio.

Potential strategies include:

- limiting underwriting in high-loss regions
- increasing premiums for high-risk properties
- promoting growth in lower-risk segments

### Introduce Risk Segmentation Models

Advanced segmentation techniques can classify policies into:

- low risk
- moderate risk
- high risk

This allows insurers to tailor pricing and underwriting decisions more effectively.

---

# 4. Regional Risk Strategy

## Problem Identified

Regional analysis indicates that **certain geographic regions experience significantly higher loss ratios and claims frequency**.

Factors influencing regional risk may include:

- weather patterns
- property density
- crime rates
- infrastructure age

## Recommended Actions

### Adjust Regional Pricing

Premiums should reflect the **true cost of insuring properties in high-risk regions**.

Regions with higher claims frequency may require increased premiums or stricter underwriting criteria.

### Improve Catastrophe Risk Modelling

Regions exposed to environmental risks such as flooding or severe storms should incorporate catastrophe risk modelling to estimate potential losses more accurately.

---

# 5. Property Age Risk Management

## Problem Identified

The **Claims Severity & Risk Drivers dashboard** shows that **older properties experience higher claims frequency**.

Older properties often contain:

- outdated plumbing systems
- aging electrical infrastructure
- structural deterioration

## Recommended Actions

### Implement Property Condition Assessments

Policies for older properties may require additional underwriting checks, such as property inspections or condition assessments.

### Adjust Premiums for Aging Infrastructure

Premium pricing should reflect the increased risk associated with older buildings.

---

# 6. Broker Performance Management

## Problem Identified

Distribution channel analysis shows that **certain brokers generate policies with higher loss ratios**, indicating weaker underwriting discipline.

## Recommended Actions

### Introduce Broker Performance Monitoring

Insurers should track key broker metrics including:

- broker loss ratio
- claims frequency
- premium volume

This allows the insurer to identify **high-performing and underperforming distribution partners**.

### Implement Broker Incentive Structures

Performance-based incentives can encourage brokers to prioritise **profitable business rather than volume alone**.
