# Risk Mitigation Strategy

## Overview

Risk mitigation refers to the actions taken by insurers to reduce the likelihood or severity of insurance claims.  
While underwriting controls which risks are accepted, risk mitigation focuses on **reducing the probability that claims occur**.

The analytics insights from the Palthanio Home Insurance Analytics project highlight several key risk drivers that insurers should address through preventative strategies.

---

# Key Risk Drivers Identified

The project identified several factors contributing to claims:

| Risk Driver | Impact |
|------|------|
| Escape of water | High frequency claims |
| Storm damage | Environmental exposure |
| Older housing infrastructure | Increased structural failures |
| High-value properties | Higher claims severity |

Mitigating these risks can significantly reduce overall claims costs.

---

# Preventative Risk Programs

## Smart Water Detection Systems

Escape of water claims are one of the largest drivers of insurance losses.

Preventative technologies include:

- smart leak detection sensors
- automatic water shutoff systems
- plumbing monitoring devices

These technologies can detect leaks early and prevent extensive damage.

---

## Property Maintenance Awareness

Many claims arise from poor property maintenance.

Insurance companies can encourage policyholders to:

- regularly inspect plumbing systems
- maintain roofing structures
- inspect electrical systems
- repair structural damage early

Educational programs can help reduce claim frequency.

---

## Weather Risk Preparedness

Storm and environmental damage can significantly impact insurance claims.

Mitigation strategies include:

- strengthening roof structures
- installing storm-resistant materials
- improving drainage systems
- protecting properties against flood risk

Proactive protection can reduce catastrophic losses.

---

# Incentive Programs

Insurance companies can encourage preventative behaviour by offering incentives such as:

- premium discounts for leak detection systems
- reduced premiums for home safety upgrades
- discounts for smart home monitoring systems

These incentives promote risk reduction while improving customer engagement.

---

# Monitoring High-Risk Segments

Insurers should monitor segments that demonstrate elevated risk characteristics, including:

- older properties
- high-value rebuild costs
- properties located in severe weather zones

Continuous monitoring helps insurers adjust strategies as risk conditions change.

---

# Expected Benefits

Implementing effective risk mitigation strategies can result in:

| Benefit | Description |
|------|------|
| Reduced claims frequency | Fewer claim events |
| Lower claims severity | Reduced repair costs |
| Improved customer safety | Better property protection |
| Improved portfolio profitability | Reduced claims expenditure |

---

# Conclusion

Risk mitigation is a critical component of insurance operations.  
By encouraging preventative behaviour and leveraging technology to reduce risk exposure, insurers can significantly lower the financial impact of claims.

Combining strong underwriting policies with proactive risk mitigation programs helps insurers maintain **long-term portfolio stability and profitability**.
